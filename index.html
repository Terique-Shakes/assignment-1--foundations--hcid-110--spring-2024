    LeBron James was born in Akron, Ohio, on December 30, 1984. He had a difficult upbringing filled with financial struggles. LeBron was raised by Gloria James, a single mother, and frequently moved between apartments in Akron's high-crime neighborhoods. Despite hardships, he developed a quick skill on the basketball court and attracted attention from an early age. LeBron was a basketball prodigy at St. Vincent-St. Mary High School, where he excelled tremendously. He made the historic transition from high school to the NBA in 2003 after the Cleveland Cavaliers selected him with the first overall pick.
